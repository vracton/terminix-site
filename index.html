Our Mission

Terminix was designed with one clear purpose: to protect Earth from unpredictable cosmic threats that could cause catastrophic damage to our planet and civilization.

By deploying advanced detection systems and neutralization technology, Terminix acts as Earth's first line of defense against asteroids, comets, and other stellar objects on collision courses.

GLOBAL PROTECTION INITIATIVE

Advanced Defense Features

Terminix is equipped with six specialized systems designed to detect, analyze, and neutralize potential threats.

Quantum Detection Array

Advanced sensors capable of detecting objects as small as 1 meter across at distances up to 10 million kilometers from Earth.

Fusion Pulse Cannons

High-energy weapons designed to fragment or vaporize approaching objects with precision targeting and minimal debris.

AI Threat Analysis

Autonomous system that evaluates approaching objects, calculates impact probability, and prioritizes response protocols.

Gravitational Diverter

Creates localized gravity wells to alter the trajectory of larger objects that cannot be safely destroyed.

Self-Replicating Drones

Small autonomous units that can be deployed to analyze composition of objects and deliver targeted countermeasures.

Global Alert Network

Communications system that coordinates with Earth-based facilities and provides real-time updates on potential threats.

Frequently Asked Questions

Terminix uses a combination of advanced radar, lidar, and infrared sensors that can detect objects of various sizes and compositions. The quantum detection array is sensitive enough to identify objects as small as 1 meter across at significant distances, giving ample time for response planning.
The fusion pulse cannons are calibrated to break threats into fragments small enough to burn up completely in Earth's atmosphere. For larger objects, the gravitational diverter can alter trajectories to ensure debris follows safe paths away from Earth.
Terminix is operated by an international consortium of space agencies and governed by a multinational oversight committee. While the system has significant autonomous capabilities, all major defensive actions require human authorization from multiple authorities.
The system is designed to protect against 99.8% of potential asteroid and comet threats. The only limitations are extremely large objects (over 1km in diameter) discovered with very little warning time. However, such objects are continuously monitored by ground-based telescopes, making sudden appearances unlikely.
